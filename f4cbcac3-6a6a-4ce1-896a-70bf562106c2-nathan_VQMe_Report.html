Why Forcing Yourself Hasn't Created Lasting Growth - The TEAR Model

Most business owners try to fix revenue problems by changing their actions—new marketing strategies, different pricing models, more aggressive sales tactics. But you can't sustain new business behaviors without addressing the thoughts and emotions driving your current approach.

You already know what you should do to grow revenue. But you don't do it consistently. The reason? You don't feel like it.

That feeling—that emotion—is the step most people skip. They jump straight from knowing what business actions to take to trying to force those actions, completely bypassing the energy required to sustain them.

So you try to force yourself to follow through on growth strategies. And it works—for a while. But rarely consistently. Why? Forcing action without shifting the underlying thought pattern leaves you split: your business activities are in one place while your head and your heart feel like they're in another. Ever felt that disconnect when trying to execute on revenue growth?

Your Pattern in Action:

When you're faced with a revenue decision—whether it's pricing, investment, or growth strategy—your brain immediately thinks: "I need to figure this out on my own. Taking advice will limit my options and freedom."

That thought creates emotions of resistance, isolation, and pressure.

Those emotions drive actions like avoiding input from advisors, dismissing market feedback, or making decisions in a vacuum.

The result? Revenue strategies that might be technically sound but miss crucial market insights, pricing that doesn't reflect true value, or investment decisions made without the collaborative input that could multiply their effectiveness.

Meanwhile, that pattern of biased thinking prevents you from accessing your incredible assets for making courageous decisions based on your values while remaining open to the perspectives that could accelerate your growth.
