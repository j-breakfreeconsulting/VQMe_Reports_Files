Why Changing Your Actions Hasn't Worked - The TEAR Model

Most career development advice focuses on actions: "Network more," "Speak up in meetings," "Ask for feedback," "Volunteer for stretch assignments." But you can't sustain new actions without addressing the thoughts and emotions driving your current behavior.

You already know what you should do. But you don't do it consistently. The reason? You don't feel like it.

That feeling—that emotion—is the step most people skip in career development. They jump straight from knowing what to do to trying to force the action, completely bypassing the energy required to sustain it.

Thoughts → Emotion → Action → Results

Your thoughts create emotion (energy in motion). That emotion provides the energy to take action, which leads to your results.

So you try to force yourself. And it works—for a while. But rarely consistently. Why? Forcing action without shifting the underlying thought pattern leaves you split: your body is in one place while your head and your heart feel like they're in another. Ever felt that disconnect?

Your Pattern in Action:

When you see an advancement opportunity or leadership development program, your brain immediately thinks: "They're just going to try to control me and tell me what to do. I already know what I'm doing."

That thought creates emotions of resistance and frustration.

Those emotions drive actions like avoiding the opportunity, not applying, or participating halfheartedly.

The result? You stay where you are, reinforcing the story that advancement isn't really for you or that the organization doesn't see your value.

Meanwhile, that pattern of biased thinking prevents you from accessing your incredible assets for building the relationships and seeking the input that actually accelerate leadership development.
