Every productivity system focuses on changing your actions. But you can't sustain new actions without addressing the thoughts and emotions driving your current behavior.

That feeling—that emotion—is the step most people skip. They jump straight from knowing what to do to trying to force the action, completely bypassing the energy required to sustain it.

So you try to force yourself to delegate. You try to force boundaries. And it works—for a while. But rarely consistently. Why? Forcing action without shifting the underlying thought pattern leaves you split: your body is implementing the system while your head and your heart feel like you're losing control.
