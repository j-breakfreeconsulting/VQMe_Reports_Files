Why Forcing Yourself Hasn't Created Lasting Change - The TEAR Model

Most people trying to improve revenue focus on changing their actions—new pricing structures, different marketing approaches, more aggressive sales tactics. But sustainable change follows a specific sequence:

Thoughts → Emotion → Action → Results

Your thoughts create emotion (energy in motion). That emotion provides the energy to take action, which leads to your results.

When most people try to change their results, they focus on changing their actions. But you can't sustain new actions without addressing the thoughts and emotions driving your current behavior.

You already know what you should do. But you don't do it. The reason? You don't feel like it.

That feeling—that emotion—is the step most people skip. They jump straight from knowing what to do to trying to force the action, completely bypassing the energy required to sustain it.

So you try to force yourself to price higher, pursue bigger opportunities, or make bolder revenue decisions. And it works—for a while. But rarely consistently. Why? Forcing action without shifting the underlying thought pattern leaves you split: your business actions are in one place while your head and your heart feel like they're in another. Ever felt that disconnect?
