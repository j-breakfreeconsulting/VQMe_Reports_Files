Why Trying Harder Keeps Backfiring - The TEAR Model

Most business owners focus on changing their actions when revenue stagnates—they try new marketing tactics, adjust pricing randomly, or work longer hours. But you can't sustain new actions without addressing the thoughts and emotions driving your current approach.

You already know what drives revenue growth. But you don't consistently do it. The reason? You don't feel like it.

That feeling—that emotion—is the step most people skip. They jump from knowing what to do (raise prices, systematize sales, invest in growth) straight to trying to force the action, completely bypassing the energy required to sustain it.

So you try to force yourself to be more strategic. And it works—for a while. But rarely consistently. Why? Forcing action without shifting the underlying thought pattern leaves you split: your body is executing tactics while your head and your heart feel like they're in another place entirely. Ever felt that disconnect in important revenue conversations?

Your Pattern in Action:

When you're facing a pricing decision or investment opportunity, your brain immediately thinks: "I shouldn't have to follow someone else's formula for this. I know my business better than any consultant or framework."

That thought creates emotions of resistance and self-sufficiency mixed with underlying doubt about whether what you're doing really matters.

Those emotions drive actions like avoiding systematic pricing strategies, researching endlessly without deciding, or defaulting to what's familiar rather than what's profitable.

The result? Revenue growth that feels harder than it should be, with profitability that never quite matches the value you know you create.

Meanwhile, that pattern of biased thinking prevents you from accessing your incredible assets for making courageous decisions that balance your values with market realities.
