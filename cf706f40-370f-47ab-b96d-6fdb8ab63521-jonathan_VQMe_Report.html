Why Forcing Yourself Hasn't Created Lasting Change - The TEAR Model

Most reputation-building advice focuses on actions: network more, share wins, speak up. But you can't sustain new actions without addressing the thoughts and emotions driving your current behavior.

You already know what you should do. But you don't do it. The reason? You don't feel like it.

That feeling—that emotion—is the step most people skip. They jump straight from knowing what to do to trying to force the action, completely bypassing the energy required to sustain it.

Thoughts → Emotion → Action → Results

Your thoughts create emotion (energy in motion). That emotion provides the energy to take action, which leads to your results.

So you try to force yourself. And it works—for a while. But rarely consistently. Why? Forcing action without shifting the underlying thought pattern leaves you split: your body is in one place while your head and your heart feel like they're in another. Ever felt that disconnect?

Your Pattern in Action:

When you're in a meeting or situation where you could build visibility, your brain immediately thinks: "What I'm doing isn't that special" or "I shouldn't put myself forward."

That thought creates emotions of discomfort, hesitation, or even guilt about self-promotion.

Those emotions drive actions like staying quiet, deflecting credit to others, or minimizing your contributions.

The result? You remain invisible while others with less capability but more confidence build the reputation you want.

Meanwhile, that pattern of biased thinking prevents you from accessing your incredible assets for standing confidently in your values while remaining genuinely open to others—exactly what creates respected reputations.
